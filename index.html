Problem Statement

Cinema chains struggle to accurately predict daily audience footfall. Demand is influenced by day-of-week patterns, holidays, booking behavior, theater location, and local demographics‚Äîcreating high variance and unpredictability.

Poor forecasting leads to: revenue leakage from understaffing, operational waste from overstaffing, suboptimal screen allocation, and missed promotional opportunities.

Methodology

5-Step ML Pipeline

1

Data Cleaning

Unified time zones, parsed datetimes, handled nulls. Merged 7 datasets on theater IDs and show dates.

2

Feature Engineering

Created 80+ features: temporal, cyclical, lag, rolling, EWM, booking aggregates, and interaction terms.

3

Preprocessing

Label encoding for categorical features. KNN Imputer (k=5, distance-weighted). StandardScaler normalization.

4

Time-Series CV

TimeSeriesSplit (5 folds) to prevent leakage and maintain chronological integrity during validation.

5

Ensemble Modeling

Trained Ridge, LightGBM, XGBoost. Combined via weighted averaging: 10% Ridge + 40% LightGBM + 50% XGBoost.

TimeSeriesSplit Cross-Validation

5-fold time-series cross-validation ensures chronological integrity and prevents data leakage. Each fold trains on past data and validates on future data.

Why TimeSeriesSplit?

  • ‚úì Respects temporal order (no future information leakage)
  • ‚úì Simulates real production scenario
  • ‚úì Reveals model stability across time periods
  • ‚úì Prevents overfitting to specific date ranges

CV Results

Both LightGBM and XGBoost showed stable cross-validation scores with low variance, confirming the model generalizes well across different time periods.

Mean CV R¬≤ scores consistently above 0.90, indicating robust forecasting performance.

Challenges Solved

Data Leakage Prevention

Implemented TimeSeriesSplit validation and shifted all rolling windows to ensure only past data informed each prediction. No future information leaked into training.

üìâ

Non-Stationarity

Audience patterns evolve across seasons. Rolling statistics, EWM, and trend features dynamically adjusted for changing demand patterns over time.

üî¢

High Cardinality

Thousands of unique theater IDs created sparsity. Label encoding + theater-level aggregates reduced dimensionality while preserving entity identity.

Overfitting Mitigation

L1/L2 regularization (reg_alpha, reg_lambda) plus ensemble blending balanced bias-variance tradeoffs, improving generalization to unseen theaters.
